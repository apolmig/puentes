Civic clarity that fits the feed

Turn dense public info into source-linked posts, scripts, and prompts young people will actually open.

Puentes is an open agentic AI system for young people, educators, youth groups, and trusted creators. It turns dense civic info into carousel packs, short-video scripts, classroom prompts, and creator-ready explainers that feel familiar without dropping nuance. No panic bait. No wall-of-text PDF energy. Every draft keeps receipts, flags manipulation, and waits for human review.

Problem

Public information shows up as dense docs while the feed rewards outrage, certainty, and identity conflict.

Response

Puentes turns the same civic material into carousel posts, short scripts, and prompts that keep context attached.

Principle

It does not tell young people what to think. It helps them see what is true, what is missing, and what is being manipulated.

Creator lens

Built for student creators, youth orgs, classroom teams, and trusted messengers who need better civic media tools.

The system is designed to feel familiar to Gen Z and younger audiences without becoming cringe or careless. It uses plain language, strong hooks, and format-native structure while keeping sources visible and uncertainty honest.

Feels native

Short, scrollable, and clear

Outputs are shaped for carousel swipes, 30-45 second explainers, and discussion prompts people can actually use.

Keeps receipts

Source-linked by default

Every claim stays tied to reporting, documents, or community questions so creators are not forced into vague summary mode.

Protects tone

No rage-bait voice

Puentes flags fear hooks, false binaries, dog whistles, and pressure to perform certainty just to win engagement.

Ready for youth spaces

Works across feed, class, and community

One reviewed packet can become a creator caption, a short script, a classroom prompt set, and a youth workshop starter.

              Safeguards

              Built to reduce manipulation, not just make civic content faster.

              Manipulation detection

              Flags fear hooks, false binaries, scapegoating, and outrage packaging designed to trigger identity-first reactions.

              Amplification awareness

              Highlights when a claim or clip is spreading because it is algorithmically loud, not because it is well sourced.

              Human review gate

              Every output is handoff-ready, not publish-ready. Teachers, editors, and trusted creators still make the final call.

              Nonpartisan framing

              Prompts and outputs are designed to widen understanding, not optimize for clout, ideological victory, or false balance.

              System blueprint

              A practical architecture for open civic media production.

              01

              Source graph

              Documents, reporting, creator prompts, and community questions are indexed with provenance, date, and trust metadata.

              02

              Claim workflows

              Claims are decomposed, checked against evidence, and marked as supported, contested, or still unresolved.

              03

              Rhetoric lens

              Language is scanned for manipulation patterns, polarization signals, and youth-safety concerns before reuse.

              04

              Format engine

              Reviewed facts become carousels, short scripts, creator captions, and classroom tools while citations stay attached.

              05

              Review workspace

              Humans inspect sources, tone, omissions, and risk notes before approving material for real audiences.

              06

              Open governance

              The system stays auditable through transparent prompts, source policies, and documented moderation constraints.
