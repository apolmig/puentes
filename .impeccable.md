## Design Context

### Users
Puentes is used by trusted creators, educators, and youth organizers who need to turn fast-moving civic confusion into source-linked explainers for young audiences. They are usually working under time pressure and need to move from a noisy question to a clear, credible handoff without getting lost in a complex interface.

### Brand Personality
Elegant, lucid, youth-native. The interface should feel calm, modern, and confident rather than institutional, loud, or startup-generic. It should earn trust through restraint and clarity while still feeling current enough to appeal to Gen Z creators.

### Aesthetic Direction
Light-first editorial minimalism with sharper social-native energy. Warm paper-toned backgrounds, ink-heavy typography, precise accent color, and selective motion. The experience should feel closer to an intelligent magazine layout or premium creative tool than a civic dashboard. Avoid glassmorphism, neon tech aesthetics, purple gradients, bloated card stacks, and corporate SaaS visual patterns.

### Design Principles
1. Lead with one clear action at a time and reduce competing visual noise.
2. Make trust visible through structure, citations, and review flow instead of heavy explanation.
3. Keep the interface elegant and lightweight, with strong hierarchy and fewer nested containers.
4. Balance credibility with youth appeal through refined typography, sharp copy, and contemporary composition.
5. Design mobile as a first-class experience, not a compressed desktop afterthought.
