P Puentes Open Agentic AI for Youth Civic Information Integrity
Mission Workflow Outputs Safeguards

Civic clarity for algorithmic spaces

Turn public information into youth-ready media without turning up the heat.

Puentes is an open agentic AI system that helps young people, educators, and trusted creators translate complex civic issues into source-linked explainers, short-form scripts, classroom prompts, and community discussion tools. It detects manipulation, surfaces amplification risk, and keeps a human reviewer in control before anything is published.

Problem

Institutions publish dense civic information while platforms reward outrage, speed, and identity conflict.

Response

Puentes makes public issues understandable, source-linked, and safer for youth-facing discussion spaces.

Principle

It does not tell people what to think. It helps them understand, verify, and discuss with more context.

How it works

An agentic workflow built for integrity, not engagement hacking.

Each stage is explicit and reviewable. Select a stage to inspect what Puentes does, what it produces, and where humans stay in the loop.

Input sources

Gather civic material from places young people rarely have time to parse.

Puentes ingests public documents, trusted reporting, and questions from communities or classrooms, then structures them into traceable source packets instead of a loose prompt history.

  • Public documents and institutional releases
  • Reliable reporting and local context
  • Community and classroom question intake

Outputs

One verified source base, multiple youth-facing formats.

The same reviewed information can be adapted for different messengers and settings without losing citations, nuance, or tone controls.

Youth-facing media

Short, source-linked content that explains without flattening.

Puentes can draft explainers, carousel slides, short-video scripts, and discussion prompts in language that respects young audiences without sounding institutional or patronizing.

  • Carousel outlines with citations
  • Short-video scripts with narration beats
  • Context cards for comments and group chats

Safeguards

Built to reduce manipulation, not just summarize content faster.

Manipulation detection

Flags emotionally coercive language, false binaries, scapegoating, and bait designed to trigger tribal reactions.

Amplification awareness

Highlights when a claim or clip may be benefiting from algorithmic acceleration rather than evidentiary quality.

Human review gate

Every output is review-ready, not publish-ready. Editors and educators remain accountable decision makers.

Nonpartisan framing

Prompts, outputs, and review rubrics are designed to widen understanding instead of optimize for ideological victory.

System blueprint

A practical architecture for open civic media production.

01

Source graph

Documents, reporting, and community questions are indexed with provenance, date, and trust metadata.

02

Claim workflows

Claims are decomposed, retrieved against evidence, and scored for support, conflict, or uncertainty.

03

Rhetoric lens

Language is scanned for manipulation patterns, polarization signals, and youth-safety concerns before reuse.

04

Format engine

Approved facts are transformed into scripts, explainers, prompts, and educator tools with retained citations.

05

Review workspace

Humans inspect sources, tone, omissions, and risk notes before approving adaptation for real audiences.

06

Open governance

The system stays auditable through transparent prompts, source policies, and documented moderation constraints.

Who benefits

Students, youth organizations, educators, and community creators who need better civic media tools.

Through Energia en Accion's mentoring networks, school partnerships, NGOs, and challenge-based learning programs, Puentes can help smaller institutions produce reliable content that can actually travel in youth media environments.
